## Interpreting the Key Metrics

- **Historical Return:** These figures reflect *past* performance and are strong across the core holdings (e.g., AAPL annualized ~30.6%, AXON ~37.4%). However, past returns do not guarantee future results.
- **Volatility:** Measures how much an asset's price fluctuates. Large caps like JNJ (~18.6%) are relatively stable, whereas growth names like CELH (~68.6%) swing sharply.
- **Sharpe Ratio:** Return earned per unit of risk. Higher is better—AAPL's 1.05 is excellent, while lower Sharpe names like CROX (0.50) contribute return potential at higher relative risk.
- **Max Drawdown:** The largest peak-to-trough loss historically observed. Several growth names have experienced drawdowns of 60–90%, underscoring why they are held at smaller weights.
- **CAPM Expected Return:** A *forward-looking* estimate of return based on each asset's sensitivity to market risk (beta). Higher-beta names like CROX (11.5%) and AMZN (11.1%) carry higher expected returns—but also higher risk—while low-beta defensives like JNJ (5.6%) offer lower expected returns in exchange for stability.
- **Momentum Outlook (SMA Trend):** By comparing the 50-day and 200-day moving averages, we gauge near-term trend direction. Names trading above both SMAs (e.g., AAPL, GOOGL, AMZN, JPM, CROX) show positive momentum, while those below (e.g., MSFT, AXON, CELH, HD) show weaker near-term trends and were generally trimmed accordingly.

The portfolio deliberately balances these three lenses—**historical performance, CAPM expected return, and momentum**—rather than relying on any single measure.

## Interpreting the Key Metrics

- **Historical Return:** Reflects past performance over the analyzed period (~2,500 trading days). Names like CELH and AXON delivered outsized historical returns, though with correspondingly higher volatility.
- **Volatility:** Measures the variability of returns. The large-cap core (e.g., AAPL, MSFT ~27–29%) is notably calmer than the growth sleeve (e.g., CELH ~69%, APPS ~94%), which is why the riskier names carry smaller weights.
- **Sharpe Ratio:** Measures return per unit of risk. Higher values (NVDA, AAPL) indicate more efficient risk-adjusted performance and are rewarded with larger allocations.
- **Maximum Drawdown:** Shows the worst peak-to-trough decline. The most severe drawdowns (many growth names exceeding -70%) informed the decision to size those positions conservatively.
- **CAPM Expected Return:** A forward-looking estimate based on each asset's beta (sensitivity to the broader market). Higher-beta names like NVDA and ELF carry higher CAPM expectations, but this comes hand-in-hand with greater downside sensitivity in market declines. This metric is theoretical and complements — rather than replaces — historical data.
- **Momentum Outlook (SMA Trend):** When the 50-day moving average sits above the 200-day, it signals near-term positive momentum ("golden cross"); the reverse suggests weakening trend. This signal was used to tilt weights toward names in confirmed uptrends (NVDA, AAPL, GOOGL, CROX, JPM) and to trim those in downtrends (AMZN, MSFT, CELH, ELF, AXON's recent softening).
